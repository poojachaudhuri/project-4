Bronx leads city in heat and hot water complaints. One building has an outsized share of complaints.

By Pooja Chaudhuri

April 6, 2023


An analysis of 311 complaints made to the city between 2020-2022, reveals that complaints related to heat and hot water have increased in the last three years. But they are not evenly distribited. In 2022, Bronx accounted for more than a third of the total complaints.

Out of the top 10 addresses with the highest number of complaints in 2022, seven were in the Bronx. However, one address in Northwest Bronx - 2176 Tiebout Avenue - had a vastly outsized share, with over 3.000 complaints.

According to a report from the city comptroller’s office released earlier in January, Northwest Bronx is home to privately-owned buildings with some of the most persistent heat complaints.




Nine percent of all 96,178 complaints from the Bronx in 2022 came from just one zip code in Northwest Bronx – 10457.

Zip code 10457 has a large population density and the median income of the zip code ($42,639) is lower than the median income of the Bronx ($43,011). An analysis of 311 service requests related to heat and hot water, collected from NYC Open Data, shows that zip codes that have a lower median income tend to register higher complaints related to heat and hot water.

The vastly outsized complaints from the zip code can be traced to 2176 Tiebout Avenue – which accounts for more than a third of all the complaints.

“They [the city] turn on the heating at 6am for some time,” an elderly woman who lives on the first floor of 2176 Tiebout Avenue said. She claimed that she suffers from lupus and her medical condition makes it difficult to cope with the cold. “Last year, I had pneumonia and they did not turn on the heating. I use this…” she said pointing at the portable heater in her living room.

The city comptroller had acknowledged in his report that a consistently cold home has been proven to cause negative health impacts, particularly for individuals with existing health conditions, children, and the elderly.

The resident, who did not wish to be named, said that she has been living in the apartment for 34 years and lamented that the landlord neglects complaints. “My niece lives on the second floor of this building. She has three children and has to turn on the oven to keep the apartment warm,” she claimed. When asked whether she has made 311 complaints, the woman said that she has but it hasn’t been useful.

However, two other residents of the building who also live on the first floor, had a different story to tell.

“I have been living here for 28 years and have not faced an issue,” said one of them, while the other added, “My family has lived in this same building for 50 years and we have not faced an issue.”

Both the residents, who are also senior citizens, did not wish to be named. They told this reporter that 2176 Tiebout Avenue has 20 apartments and the heating is divided into four lines. They claimed that the heating on their line works just fine.

The superintendent of the building Ernesto claimed that the complaints have all been made by one resident alone – the elderly woman who stays on the first floor.

However, an analysis of the Housing Preservation and Development's response to the complaints shows that multiple residents have made complaints about the heating.

A woman who lives in the neighbouring building also said that that 2176 Tiebout Avenue has been facing issues for a long time.

Earlier this year, the HPD said in a statement to The City , "We always respond to complaints and take the appropriate action when unsafe conditions are discovered. We are reviewing all of the findings and will assess the feasibility of the report’s recommendations on code enforcement."
